================================================================================
DATA DICTIONARY
Future Feelings Experiment — Analysis Datasets
================================================================================

This document describes the variables in the two analysis datasets:

  01_data/analysis/df_wide.csv / .RData   (1,895 rows x 178 columns)
  01_data/analysis/df_long.csv / .RData   (15,160 rows x 136 columns)

The wide dataset has one row per participant. The long dataset has one row per
conjoint candidate per trial per participant (4 trials x 2 candidates = 8 rows
per participant). Both datasets share the same participant-level variables; the
long dataset additionally contains trial- and candidate-level conjoint variables.

POMP scores (Percent of Maximum Possible) rescale a composite to the range
[0, 1], where 0 = minimum possible score and 1 = maximum possible score.
All scale composites are provided both as raw sums and as POMP scores.

Variables marked [WIDE ONLY] appear only in df_wide.
Variables marked [LONG ONLY] appear only in df_long.
All other variables appear in both datasets.

4. CONJOINT EXPERIMENT — WIDE FORMAT [WIDE ONLY]
================================================================================

The conjoint experiment presented participants with 4 choice tasks (trials).
In each trial, they saw two hypothetical political candidates (A and B) and
chose which one they would vote for. Each candidate profile was defined by
5 attributes: sex, age, party, democratic behaviour, and economic reputation.

For each trial (1–4) and each candidate (1 = Candidate A, 2 = Candidate B),
the following raw profile attributes are stored:

exp_choiceT_sexC        Sex of candidate C in trial T
                        Values: Male, Female

exp_choiceT_ageC        Age of candidate C in trial T
                        Values: integer (30–69)

exp_choiceT_partyC      Party affiliation of candidate C in trial T
                        Values: Conservatives, Labour, Liberal Democrats,
                                Green Party, Reform UK

exp_choiceT_demC_raw    Randomly assigned democratic behaviour description
                        for candidate C in trial T (raw label from the design)

exp_choiceT_demC        Democratic behaviour of candidate C in trial T
                        (coded from the raw label)
                        Values: democratic, undemocratic

exp_choiceT_ecoC        Economic reputation of candidate C in trial T
                        Values: Good at handling economic matters,
                                Neither good nor bad reputation on economic
                                matters,
                                Bad at handling economic matters

exp_conjointT           Which candidate the participant chose in trial T
                        Values: Candidate A, Candidate B

(T = trial number 1–4; C = candidate number 1–2)


================================================================================
5. CONJOINT EXPERIMENT — LONG FORMAT [LONG ONLY]
================================================================================

In the long dataset each row represents one candidate within one trial for one
participant. The conjoint profile attributes and the participant's choice are
stored in the following variables:

trial                   Conjoint trial number (1–4)

candidate               Candidate number within the trial (1 = A, 2 = B)

candidate_label         Candidate label
                        Values: Candidate A, Candidate B

choice                  Which candidate the participant chose in this trial
                        Values: Candidate A, Candidate B

conjoint_choice         Whether this candidate was chosen (binary outcome
                        variable used in conjoint models)
                        Values: 0 = not chosen, 1 = chosen

conjoint_sex            Sex of this candidate
                        Values: Female, Male

conjoint_age            Age of this candidate (integer, 30–69)

conjoint_party          Party affiliation of this candidate
                        Values: Conservatives, Labour, Liberal Democrats,
                                Green Party, Reform UK

conjoint_dem            Democratic behaviour of this candidate
                        Values: democratic, undemocratic

conjoint_eco            Economic reputation of this candidate
                        Values: Good at handling economic matters,
                                Neither good nor bad reputation on economic
                                matters,
                                Bad at handling economic matters

6. PRE-TREATMENT SCALES — COMPOSITES
================================================================================

All composites are stored as both a raw sum and a POMP score (0–1).

polpart_pomp            Pre-treatment political participation composite
                        (POMP, 0–1); 5 items
polpart_sum             Raw sum of the 5 political participation items
polpart_party           Item: member of or active in a political party
polpart_badge           Item: worn a badge or displayed a sticker
polpart_petition        Item: signed a petition
polpart_demonstration   Item: attended a demonstration
polpart_social_media    Item: posted political content on social media

aut_pomp                Authoritarian preferences composite (POMP, 0–1); 3 items
aut_sum                 Raw sum of the 3 authoritarian preference items
aut_strong_leader       Item: support for a strong leader above parliament
aut_strong_government   Item: support for a strong government without opposition
aut_less_control        Item: support for less government control

dem_pomp                Democratic preferences composite (POMP, 0–1); 3 items
dem_sum                 Raw sum of the 3 democratic preference items
dem_equal_vote          Item: equal voting rights are important
dem_fundamental_rights  Item: protection of fundamental rights is important
dem_multiple_parties    Item: multiple parties are important for democracy

cognitive_reappraisal_pomp   Cognitive reappraisal composite (POMP, 0–1)
                             ERQ subscale; 6 items
cognitive_reappraisal_sum    Raw sum
erq_reappraisal1 – erq_reappraisal6   Individual ERQ reappraisal items

expressive_suppression_pomp  Expressive suppression composite (POMP, 0–1)
                             ERQ subscale; 4 items
expressive_suppression_sum   Raw sum
erq_suppression1 – erq_suppression4   Individual ERQ suppression items

ambiguity_intolerance_pomp   Intolerance of ambiguity composite (POMP, 0–1);
                             4 items
ambiguity_intolerance_sum    Raw sum
ambiguity1 – ambiguity4      Individual ambiguity intolerance items

aot_pomp                Actively open-minded thinking composite (POMP, 0–1);
                        6 items (reverse-coded versions used in composite)
aot_sum                 Raw sum (using reverse-coded items)
aot1 – aot6             Individual AOT items (original direction)
aot1_rev – aot6_rev     Individual AOT items (reverse-coded)
